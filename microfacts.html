Bacteriology
Master the Gram stain first — it halves your differential instantly. Then layer virulence factors, classic clinical syndromes, and treatment. High-yield sequences: cocci → rods; GP → GN; aerobic → anaerobic. Know the one-liner pearl for each organism cold.
🧬
Virology
Group by genome type: RNA vs DNA, ss vs ds, +sense vs −sense, enveloped vs naked. The Baltimore classification is your scaffold. For each virus know its tropism, classic syndrome, and distinguishing lab finding. Enveloped = ether-labile = killed by alcohol.
🪱
Parasitology
Organize by habitat: intestinal protozoa → blood/tissue protozoa → helminths. Vector associations are heavily tested (mosquito/tick/fly/snail). Eosinophilia = helminth tissue migration. Treatment is narrow: know metronidazole, chloroquine, praziquantel, ivermectin, and albendazole targets.
🍄
Mycology
Divide into systemic dimorphic fungi vs opportunistic fungi. Dimorphic rule: mold in cold (environment), yeast in heat (body). Geography matters — Histo (Ohio/Mississippi River Valley), Cocci (Southwest desert), Blasto (Great Lakes/Mississippi). Opportunists = immunocompromised hosts (HIV, transplant, steroids).
