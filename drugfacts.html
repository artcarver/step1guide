Autonomic Pharmacology
Parasympathetic (cholinergic) and sympathetic (adrenergic) drug classes. Master receptor subtypes first — M1/M2/M3, α1/α2, β1/β2 — and the drugs fall into place. High-yield for organ-specific side effects and clinical applications.
♥
Cardiovascular & Renal Pharmacology
The most tested drug system on Step 1. Know ACEi/ARBs cold — they appear in HF, HTN, CKD, and post-MI vignettes. Antiarrhythmic classes: think ion channels first, then drugs. Diuretic electrolyte effects are guaranteed questions.
🩸
Blood & Inflammation
Anticoagulants appear on nearly every Step 1 exam — know reversal agents cold. Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) is a classic trap. NSAIDs are the most-prescribed drugs in medicine; their mechanism and GI/renal toxicity are ubiquitous.
🧠
Neuro & Psych
Psych block is massive on Step 1. Memorize antidepressant side effect profiles — TCA overdose, SSRI serotonin syndrome, and bupropion seizures appear constantly. Antipsychotic EPS and NMS are classic vignettes. Lithium toxicity has a narrow therapeutic window.
🦠
Antimicrobial Pharmacology
Organized by mechanism: cell wall inhibitors → protein synthesis → DNA/folate → cell membrane. For each drug know its unique toxicity, not just its coverage. Antibiotic-associated C. diff (clindamycin, fluoroquinolones, cephalosporins) is a perennial vignette.
🍽️
Gastrointestinal & Motility
Focus on acid suppression, motility agents, and mucosal protection. High-yield for C. difficile and metabolic interactions.
🧬
Endocrine & Reproductive
Master the hypothalamic-pituitary axes, diabetes management, and reproductive hormones. High-yield for Step 1 feedback loops.
⚠️
Drug Toxicology & Antidotes
Crucial reversal agents and toxidromes. Focus on the mechanism of toxicity and the logic of the chosen antidote.
🫁
Smooth Muscle
Asthma step therapy is heavily tested — know which drugs are for acute relief vs. maintenance. Nitrate tolerance (Monday disease) is a classic Step 1 vignette. Antihistamine receptor subtypes (H1 vs H2) determine organ effects and drug indication.
⚗
Antineoplastics
Step 1 tests toxicities more than mechanisms. Key rule: every chemo drug has one unique, memorable toxicity — bleomycin → pulmonary fibrosis, cisplatin → nephrotoxicity/ototoxicity, vincristine → peripheral neuropathy (NOT bone marrow suppression). Learn these cold.
